Konni EcoTourism



Adoor Prakash, an MLA in Kerala, India, supports eco-tourism in Konni, it could be because eco-tourism promotes sustainable and responsible tourism that benefits both the environment and the local communities. Konni is a beautiful forested area in Kerala that is home to a variety of wildlife and flora. By promoting eco-tourism in Konni, Adoor Prakash may be advocating for a type of tourism that respects and preserves the natural resources of the area while providing economic benefits to the local people.Prakash was instrumental in the development of the Konni Elephant Training Centre, which is a major tourist attraction in the region. He also supported the development of other ecotourism activities, such as trekking, bamboo rafting, and wildlife safaris.
As a local politician, Prakash recognized the potential of ecotourism to support the local economy while also promoting sustainable development. He advocated for the creation of jobs in the ecotourism sector and supported local businesses that were committed to sustainable practices.
Prakash's efforts helped to put Konni on the map as an ecotourism destination and contributed to the region's economic growth. His legacy continues to inspire current and future leaders to support sustainable tourism practices in Konni and beyond.
